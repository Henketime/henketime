I thought I didn’t understand this album. While I was listening, I thought I didn’t understand this album. Not just because I don’t speak French. I don’t speak French. Not just because it felt unfamiliar. It felt unfamiliar. Before, during, and since, this album has made me feel a stranger. Because the album is so short, I thought I could supplement my writing with some information about Rose-Maïté. The research made me feel stranger and stranger. I can not tell you with any confidence who Rose-Maïté Erkoreka is, any more than you can tell me who your neighbor is. Your neighbor is a stranger. What I don’t understand about this album, and about Rose-Maïté, has distilled itself into a message: lasting beauty is strange. It has to be in order to stay with you.

I turn around slowly, although I can’t remember why. I see a glowing horizon, like the fog underneath a closed door. I regret what I’ve done, and am hesitant to act. Any movement in any direction feels wrong. Even scanning the horizon is an act of bravery. There is no reason for me to have to be here, but I can’t imagine being anywhere else. The floor is cold, but the air is warm, and I’m sitting and Iknow what alone is. Does love require pain?

I pull myself up, and my hands grip the wall of stone. The wind blows across my face, and the face of the rock feels it too. The pulse of the earth is slow and sad, but it keeps mine moving. Even when my body stops, everything beneath the surface roils, ultimately pulling, pulling down. My senses push against gravity, up to the vertical slice of infinity above. My destiny is to be pulled no longer.

I open the world and step through. The stage around me is attentive and judgmental. My legs become the strings on a bass, and I face another fear.

Pour another drink,, and I’ll come out. Pour another drink, and you’ll find me at the bottom. Content to stay, but content to pour out the door. Content to not know what the hell is going on.

Thank you for coming along with me, and, as always: take my words with a grain of salt; do your own research; and be careful.
